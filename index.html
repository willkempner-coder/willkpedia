Will Kempner (born June 12, 1993) is an American editor, filmmaker, savant, and enfant terrible. Born in New York, he also spent time in New Jersey. He was last seen in Ridgewood, Queens. At present, the only empirical evidence of his existence is a specific portfolio of creative projects, primarily editorial credits.

His personal work is rarely viewed or fully understood. From high-concept musical albums to short films, he tends to operate within some of the least respected mediums available today.

Found Footage Film Festival For Friends

During the height of lockdowns amid the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, Kempner organized an online film festival from the spare room of his former girlfriend's apartment. The event, titled Found Footage Film Festival For Friends, ran biweekly for approximately four months. The stated premise was that participants create short films using footage already available on their personal computers, though these guidelines were loosely enforced and frequently disregarded. Submissions were typically made on the day of exhibition.

The series concluded shortly before the onset of the United States racial unrest (2020-2023), during which promotional activity of that nature would likely have been considered in poor taste. In June 2021, as public health restrictions eased, a live installment of the festival was held in Bushwick.

Scandals [edit]

In 2026, Kempner launched WillKpedia, a personal website conceived as a parody of a public figure's Wikipedia page. Initially met with mild intrigue, the project quickly drew scrutiny when viewers realized he had used a large language model to â€œvibe codeâ€ much of the site. Public sentiment shifted from impressed to disgust. Former high school classmates, some of whom he had not spoken to in years, began publicly commenting on his posts with calculations estimating the volume of water allegedly wasted in generating the site's content.

Despite Kempner's insistence that he had conducted his own moral calculations and determined the project's environmental impact to be negligible, the controversy intensified. Rather than generating new professional opportunities, WillKpedia became a cautionary tale within his industry, contributing to a period of professional ostracization. Whereas colleagues had once lauded his editing ability, many now declined to respond to his increasingly frequent emails seeking freelance work.
