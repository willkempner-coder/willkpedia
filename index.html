Will Kempner (born June 12, 1993) is an American editor, filmmaker, artist, and producer. "Will K", as he is increasingly known, has worked across television, Hollywood, fashion campaigns, branded and branded media, and independent film. His editing portfolio includes directing and editing narrative shorts and features, as well as post-production work on series for HBO and Disney+.[1][2][3]

Biography [edit]

Born in New York, Kempner also spent time in New Jersey. He was last seen in Ridgewood, Queens, though these reports have been contested. At present, the only empirical evidence of Kempner’s existence is tied to a specific portfolio of creative projects, primarily editorial credits.

Awards and Screenings [edit]

Kempner’s creative output has been recognized through several industry grants and festival screenings. In 2015, he was the winner of the Todd Phillips Post Production Grant. He later won the 2019 Tribeca Film Festival Snapchat Story Contest (w/ Colin Garland and Ben Townsend). His work has also been selected for official screenings, including the 2021 NPCC Short Film Block and a scheduled presentation at NoBudge Live 12.
